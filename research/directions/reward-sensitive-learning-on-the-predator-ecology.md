# Direction

The convergent conclusion of iterations 1–5: open-ended intelligence needs (a)
**selection pressure for skill** (which the predator niche provides — hunting pays)
AND (b) a learning rule that converts that pressure into **within-life skill
acquisition**. The current rule is pure unsupervised covariance Hebbian (no reward),
so brains only acquire hunting skill across generations, slowly, and every
*mechanical* way of handing them predation (richer corpses, lethality, reliability,
scarcity, free reach) either explodes, suppresses, or **de-skills** it
([[mechanisms/predation-is-encounter-limited]],
[[mechanisms/selection-pressure-is-the-bottleneck-for-intelligence]]).

The missing piece: a **three-factor (energy-delta neuromodulated) Hebbian rule** —
gate the eligibility→weight update by the organism's recent energy change so
coactivations that preceded a gain (ate prey/plant) consolidate. NOT a value
function/TD/actor-critic; just a scalar from an already-persisted homeostatic
quantity. Iteration 1 tried it on the *foraging-only* ecology with an aggressive
band and it destabilized action_effectiveness — but on the **predator ecology**
(consume-on-kill, where competent hunting genuinely pays) with a **gentle band**,
within-life reward-learning should raise BOTH mi_sa and action_effectiveness — the
genuine "intelligent brains" loop. **Iteration 6 is testing exactly this**
(three-factor on consume-on-kill). If it works it is the breakthrough; if not, the
mechanism space is exhausted and the binding constraint is the metric contract
([[directions/reconsider-intelligence-metric-under-predation]],
[[findings/prey-consumption-target-is-structurally-unreachable-in-a-stable-ecology]]).
Natural follow-ups if promising: add the corpse/prey sensory channel
([[experiments/0003-sensing-corpse-salience]]) so pursuit is perceivable; tune the
neuromodulator band.
