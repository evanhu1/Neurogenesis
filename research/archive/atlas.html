NeuroGenesis research atlas · July 10–13, 2026

Eighteen rounds from NEAT to the boundary of the substrate.

We began by asking whether NEAT would keep producing more capable brains in a simple world of plants, movement, energy, and small neural nets. The later work added deliberately small laboratory tests around that world—not because they were the desired ecology, but because every apparent success had to answer a harder question: did a brain learn something genuinely new and useful, especially late in the run?

Current conclusion NEAT can add causally useful structure, and a protected residual can add real four-bit memory. No implemented algorithm sustains both novelty and competence. The actual fixed-resource executable also cannot literally emit an infinite nonrepeating tail; the remaining scientific target is operational growth through successively later tested horizons.

The vocabulary behind every claim

The research became much stricter than “fitness went up.” These definitions are the contract I used to decide whether an experiment was progress, a useful mechanism, or another attractive failure.

NEAT

NeuroEvolution of Augmenting Topologies: an outer-loop algorithm that evolves neural-network weights and can add nodes or connections. Here it mutates and selects genomes between deterministic simulation episodes.

Genome, brain, behavior

A genome encodes the inherited controller. It is expressed into a runtime neural network. Behavior is the resulting sensory → internal state → action trace in the world—not the genome’s size or syntax.

Outer-loop evolution

Selection, mutation, crossover, and replacement occur between episodes. The simulation itself is a clonal evaluator; changing a genome is not an in-world learning event.

Lifetime learning

Hebbian plasticity changes synaptic state while one organism lives. Recurrent activations also carry short-term memory. A lifetime-learning claim needs carry/freeze/reset controls, not just different end behavior.

Fitness and competence

Fitness is the number selection optimizes. Competence is demonstrated task ability: finding food, retaining energy, solving a conditional program, or exploiting an ecology on held-out contexts. They are not automatically the same.

Novelty

A behavior is novel only after quotienting away IDs, absolute position, clock phase, repeated no-ops, and diagnostic bookkeeping. A growing archive or larger network is not behavioral novelty by itself.

Open-endedness

The desired conjunction: genuinely new behavior continues to appear late, remains useful, and expands rather than replaces the competent repertoire. Diversity without adaptation and a lone improving champion both fail.

Tail evidence

The last portion of a run matters more than its beginning. A one-time jump followed by a plateau is bounded novelty. Discovery must continue across successively later blocks and multiple seeds.

Deterministic seed

Fixed configuration plus seed gives the same run. A seed is one outer replicate, not a source of confidence by itself. Repeating the same seed proves replay, not robustness.

Control and knockout

A control removes the proposed causal ingredient while matching everything else. A knockout deletes the newly evolved module. If performance remains, the module was ornamental or the metric was fooled.

Sealed admission

Search may inspect mutable training contexts. A candidate qualifies only on a one-shot, disjoint panel that selection never touched. “Sealed” prevents tuning the proof after seeing the answer.

Escrow and conservation

Evaluator-created tasks can accidentally mint energy. Escrow fixes the available budget in advance; a ledger then tracks organism, food, artifact, and task compartments so activity cannot be fabricated by hidden sources.

Protected residual

An accepted controller is frozen as an immutable prefix. Later search may add a residual module but cannot rewrite the old computation. Exact knockout returns the previous controller.

Public affordance

A world feature that organisms can observe and manipulate through generic sensors/actions. Public matters because evaluator-only programs can look sophisticated while brains never read or use them.

Red Queen pressure

Difficulty produced by other evolving lineages, so adaptation changes the problem faced by others. A finite opponent cohort or frozen game produces a ladder at best, not continuing reciprocal pressure.

PowerPlay (the name is misleading)

A tiny external puzzle generator, not a new species or a natural ecological process. It presented food in a required order, evolved a solver, then searched for a slightly harder order that older solvers failed.

Memory bits

Four binary left/right cues: only 16 possible patterns. The test showed the cues, erased all visible clues, waited, and checked whether the recurrent net still chose the matching action.

Program or preamble

A fixed, evaluator-authored sequence of visible cues describing a small task. It was not code written or understood in the human sense; the experiment asked only whether an evolved brain could use that cue sequence.

Energy cache

A prepaid public account of energy. In the probe, plant energy could fund a cache and a correct challenge answer could release it. The cache was meant as a test of a public artifact, not evidence of an organism-made tool.

The acceptance rule in plain language A discovery had to be absent from old controllers, mastered by the new controller, retained alongside all earlier skills, transferable under relabeling and translation, destroyed by a targeted knockout, competent in ordinary ecology, conserved in its energy ledger, and reproducible across seeds. The tail then had to keep producing such discoveries faster than the controls.
A crucial distinction

The simple world stayed the baseline; later work built test rigs around it

The original substrate is still the familiar one: a deterministic hex-grid ecology with plants, energy, movement, eating, optional attack, and NEAT-evolved neural controllers. Most later names refer to experimental harnesses used to isolate one missing ability at a time.

Original ecological simulationOrganisms see simple local signals, choose simple actions, gain or lose energy, and compete for plants. NEAT changes genomes only between episodes.
PowerPlay test rigThe evaluator temporarily places a small sequence of food targets. It is a controlled “can this brain solve a new ordered problem?” exam, not an emergent food web.
Memory test rigThe evaluator shows a few left/right cues, removes them, then checks a later action. It isolates recurrent memory from ordinary movement, food, and visible-world shortcuts.
Public-cue and cache test rigsThe evaluator shows fixed cue sequences or manages a small public energy account. These probe whether shared information/artifacts could matter; they do not mean organisms had language or tools.
Procedural-ecology test rigThe evaluator changes where a fixed budget of plants appears in response to prior consumption. It tests closed-loop environmental mechanics, but no ecology genome evolved yet.
Why build artificial tests?Because the basic ecology plateaued. A clean test can prove one causal capability—or show exactly why that capability still does not turn into open-ended ecological evolution.
Reading rule for the rest of the reportWhen a later experiment “passes,” read it as “this narrow mechanism works under its controls.” It does not mean the simple world has acquired a new general intelligence, language, tool use, or an open-ended evolutionary process.
How the thinking changed

Six research phases, each forced by the last failure

The work did not march toward a favored design. Each phase began because an adversarial control showed that the previous “progress” could be produced without the claimed mechanism.

01

Make the measurements honest

Lineage metrics, seed coverage, tail windows, genotype/phenotype identity, continuity, and per-tick energy accounting were repaired before later claims.

Turn: a beautiful metric is worthless if stale fields or missing intervals can manufacture it.
02

Ask what NEAT itself contributes

Structural mutation produced causal nodes and connections, but fixed topology reached comparable competence. Novelty archives expanded while endpoint novelty regressed.

Turn: representation capacity works; it is not the source of continuing demand.
03

Try better objectives and opponents

Relative survival, late weighting, anchors, predation, historical crossplay, and renewable opponents each exposed a different bounded or exploitable signal.

Turn: optimizing harder cannot replace a problem source that keeps changing.
04

Force genuinely new controller state

PowerPlay created sequential tasks; protected residuals made later computation non-diluting; delayed-copy programs proved real recurrent memory.

Turn: causal capacity can grow while ordinary ecological competence gets worse.
05

Move task structure into public ecology

Visible preambles, protected decoders, and proof-carrying caches tested whether controllers could inherit and exploit public information made by other branches.

Turn: evaluator-readable syntax is not organism-readable behavior.
06

Test environment generation itself

A behavior-responsive procedural ecology passed narrow mechanics gates, but remained evaluator-owned, bounded, and unevolved. The broader family remains untested.

Turn: the missing object is an endogenous public affordance, not another hidden program.
Novel and capableNew traces must solve something old controllers cannot, while retaining ordinary survival and historical skills.
Causal, not correlatedExact residual, brain-state, artifact, creator, and semantic knockouts must remove the claimed advantage.
Public and transferableLater lineages must read/use the mechanism under alpha-renaming, translation, and independently rebuilt instances.
Still discovering lateAcross eight outer seeds, qualifying discoveries must continue in blocks [100,200), [200,400), and [400,800).

Round 14

The exact boundary of the claim

The phrase “an unbounded run that never stagnates” has a formal problem on an actual computer. This did not end the empirical work; it clarified what finite experiments can honestly establish.

Finite deterministic state ⇒ eventual halt or repetition

Fix the executable, configuration, seed, RAM, disk, and output budget. Let Xₜ contain every behavior-affecting world, genome, RNG, archive, and outer-loop value. Every field has finitely many bit patterns and every collection has a finite maximum size, so the complete state set is finite.

If an infinite run never halts, two complete states must eventually match: Xᵢ = Xⱼ. Determinism then forces every later state to repeat with the same period. The observable behavior tail is periodic too.

This does not predict an early plateauThe state space is enormous. The proof sets a claim boundary; it does not say repetition is reachable during any practical experiment.
An unbounded mathematical model is differentUnbounded integers, heap, and tape allow infinitely many states. But longer programs or histories can still collapse to a universal interpreter, counter, memory, or size ladder.
The empirical target is operationalShow causally new, retained, transferable ecological relations through geometrically increasing horizons, across seeds, while discovery rate remains nonzero and controls stay behind.
“Not implicit” needs a practical meaningThe later trace was absent from the early canonical repertoire, frozen early organisms cannot produce it, and a later innovation is causally necessary—even though deterministic code entails its own future.
Exact open gap

Give organisms a public affordance that later evolution can inherit and transform.

The strongest concrete candidate is persistent, energy-funded, recursively composable niche construction. Organisms would build public artifacts with generic observable ports; later lineages could exploit, modify, defend, or depend on those structures, and artifacts could become inputs to further construction. That can expand the realized causal interaction graph instead of merely lengthening a private program. It is a candidate, not a proven unique solution.

Organism-owned interfaceGeneric Construct/Interact actions and local artifact receptors. No task ID, creator ID, archive index, or evaluator truth reaches the brain.
Recursive public compositionArtifacts store or route bounded state/resources and expose ports that later artifacts consume. A fixed recipe menu is another bounded catalog.
Ecological inheritanceAdmitted structures persist into descendant environments so later lineages can create new dependencies, parasitism, signaling, tools, or division of labor.
Fatal conservationConstruction, storage, compute, maintenance, release, and garbage collection debit explicit material and energy compartments.
Causal admissionCreator, artifact, and consumer knockouts; alpha/translation/rebuild transfer; frozen-selection and no-payoff controls; rejection of inert growth and universal constructors.
Parallel untested routeA broader behavior-responsive procedural ecology may still work, but first needs organism learning, teacher regret, tracker/open-loop controls, public provenance, and increasing-horizon tail evidence.
