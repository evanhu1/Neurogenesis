# 2026-07-14-cost5-transfer40-80g

Status: completed; rejected as long-run baseline

## Question

Does the cost-5, transfer-40 predation economy continue improving through 80
generations while retaining economically profitable predation?

## Hypothesis

The 20-generation screen showed that cost 5 can place positive-net-attack
genomes in the top fitness quartile without restoring attack spam. Over 80
generations, those predatory strategies should persist or recur while survival
and harvesting competence continue improving. The hypothesis fails if predation
vanishes, becomes population-wide spam, or the late survival trajectory stalls
or regresses.

## Contract

- Code revision: captured by the batch manifest.
- Canonical config: `config/world.toml`.
- Deliberate overrides: `attack_attempt_cost=5`,
  `attack_energy_transfer=40`.
- Evolutionary seeds: `7,17,27`.
- Training world seeds: `11,29,47`.
- Held-out world seeds: none during training.
- Population: 48 genomes.
- Generations: 80 (`0..79`).
- Episode horizon: 1,000 ticks.
- Evaluation: contemporary-only triads, 12 memberships per genome, 24 opponent
  exposures, and 36 scored cases per genome.
- World: canonical 50x50 with 102 founders, 34 per lineage.
- Objective: `survival_times_relative_advantage`, ordinary mean aggregation.
- Evaluation workers: 14 total, allocated 5/5/4 across the three evolutionary
  seeds.
- Simulator work: 46,080 evaluator worlds and 46.08 million world ticks per
  evolutionary seed; 138.24 million ticks total.
- Artifact directory:
  `artifacts/research/runs/completed/2026-07-14-cost5-transfer40-80g/`.

Everything else inherited the canonical configuration.

## Measurements

- Explicit late interval: generations `40..79` inclusive.
- Champion, population mean, and median absolute survival; late slopes and
  generation-40-to-79 changes.
- Champion and population end-survival fractions as the 1,000-tick censoring
  audit.
- Per-genome private net attack balance, especially its prevalence in the top
  fitness quartile during generations `60..79`.
- Attack allocation, precision, energy received/lost/cost, income share, kills,
  distinct victims, and repeat-hit behavior.
- Total acquired energy, net energy profit, plant/prey rates, action
  effectiveness, plant capture, and spatial coverage.
- Species and topology only as search-health diagnostics.
- All three seeds reported separately; no claim of causal superiority over cost
  10 because this run had no matched control arm.

## Decision rule

Treat cost 5 / transfer 40 as a viable long-run baseline only if:

1. the 1,000-tick horizon remains sufficiently uncensored to rank late genomes;
2. at least two seeds retain positive late population-survival slopes without a
   collapsing median;
3. at least two seeds contain meaningful positive-net-attack genomes in the
   late top fitness quartile;
4. late attack allocation remains below 5% and population net energy profit
   stays positive; and
5. late progress is supported by normalized behavior and energy-flow evidence,
   not gross transfer cycling.

If censoring becomes material, the run still diagnoses the horizon but cannot
establish a late capability plateau. If predation disappears while plant
foraging improves, classify cost 5 as a temporary niche-opening mechanism, not
a sustained trophic baseline.

The validated plan contained 576 evaluator worlds per generation, 36 cases and

## Result

### Survival continued improving inside the evaluator

| Seed | Best survival g40 -> g79 | Mean survival g40 -> g79 | Mean late slope |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 7 | 0.516 -> 0.734 | 0.310 -> 0.358 | +0.00196/generation |
| 17 | 0.492 -> 0.693 | 0.320 -> 0.350 | +0.00102/generation |
| 27 | 0.406 -> 0.472 | 0.265 -> 0.333 | +0.00174/generation |

Action effectiveness, net energy profit, and plant harvesting also rose over
the late interval in every seed. Final champions covered 74-80% of the world and
obtained almost all external energy from plants. Thus the positive survival
slopes correspond to increasingly effective mobile foraging, not a static
behavior with a moving scalar observer.

### Profitable predation was temporary

Positive-net-attack top-quartile genome slots by ten-generation window were:

| Window | Seed 7 | Seed 17 | Seed 27 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 0-9 | 5 | 0 | 8 |
| 10-19 | 4 | 0 | 7 |
| 20-29 | 0 | 0 | 4 |
| 30-39 | 1 | 0 | 19 |
| 40-49 | 1 | 0 | 28 |
| 50-59 | 2 | 0 | 25 |
| 60-69 | 0 | 0 | 1 |
| 70-79 | 0 | 0 | 0 |

Seed 27 developed a substantial predatory phase through generation 59. Its last
late profitable predator appeared at generation 60, ranked second, earned
`+978.8` private net attack energy, obtained 40.0% of acquired energy from
attacks, and averaged 17.4 kills across 29.1 victims. No seed retained a
positive-net-attack top-quartile genome during generations 70-79.

All final champions had negative net attack balance and negligible prey rates.
They were plant foragers. Cost 5 therefore opened a real predatory niche, but
contemporary selection did not preserve it over the 80-generation tail.

### Attack spam stayed suppressed

Final population attack fractions were 1.06%, 0.68%, and 1.04%. Population net
energy profit remained positive in every seed, and final action effectiveness
and plant rates were high. The loss of predation was not caused by reversion to
indiscriminate reciprocal attack cycling.

### The 1,000-tick horizon became censored

Seed-7 and seed-17 champions first exceeded 5% end survival at generations 40
and 32 and ended with 50.0% and 34.9% of founders alive at the cutoff. Their
population end-survival fractions reached 4.0% and 2.5%. Seed 27 remained less
censored but still ended with 6.5% champion survival at tick 1,000.

The positive late slopes are real progress within the 1,000-tick evaluator, but
the best-genome ranking no longer resolves how long a large survivor tail could
continue beyond the cutoff.

## Interpretation and next decision

Do not adopt cost 5 / transfer 40 with a 1,000-tick horizon as the long-run
baseline.

- Gate 1 fails because the horizon is materially censored in seeds 7 and 17.
- Gate 2 passes: all three population-survival late slopes are positive and the
  medians do not broadly collapse.
- Gate 3 fails: profitable predation survives late in only seed 27 and vanishes
  from every top fitness quartile by generation 70.
- Gate 4 passes: attack output remains sparse and population profit positive.
- Gate 5 passes for plant-foraging improvement, not for sustained trophic
  evolution.

This result identifies cost 5 as a temporary niche-opening parameter rather than
a sufficient mechanism for sustained predator-forager coevolution. Any further
80-generation survival claim needs a longer common training horizon. Any attempt
to preserve predation needs a separate ecological or selection mechanism rather
than another inference from attack-cost tuning alone.
